So why is it called 'Rock Paper Science'?

The stone age probably lasted more than 3 million years and ended just around about five thousand years ago with the advent of metalworking. During this time life was generally short and unforgiving for humanity. A mere six thousand years later the printing press was invented, sparking a host of sociological revolutions and providing a basis for the scientific method. In a few short centuries we have seen life spans shoot up, standards of living rise and our collective understanding of the world around us increase hugely. Everyone living and everyone who has lived is part of that same story, to greater or lesser extents.

The three nouns Rock, Paper and Science represent stepping stones on that journey. By no means are they unique, but they are memorable.
